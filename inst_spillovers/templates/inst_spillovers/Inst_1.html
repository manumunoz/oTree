Instructions Part 1: Communication Stage

{% endblock %} {% block content %}

At the beginning of this part, the computer randomly forms groups of four participants. You will interact with the same other participants throughout Part 1 and Part 2. Part 1 consists of 20 rounds.

Decisions in each round

Before Part 1 starts, each participant will be randomly assigned a label: 1, 2, 3, or 4. You will see your label on the top in every screen. Every participant's label is fixed throughout the experiment.

In each round there are two stages: a communication stage, which consist of 2 decision-turns, and an action stage, which consists of a single decision-turn. Your earnings will depend only on the action you and the other three participants choose and not on either of the messages sent in the communication stage. This will be explained in detail as follows.

Communication stage

In the communication stage, preceding the action stage, each participant will be asked to send messages to the other three. There are two identical decision-turns in the communication stage: First and Second message. In each of them, you will observe a screen as illustrated in the image below. You can each choose one of four possible messages:

by clicking on the corresponding option (see the third panel on the right). The image illustrates the second message screen, after all players have sent their first message.



The messages have the same names and colors as the actions because they may indicate the action you intend to choose in the action stage. However, you do not have to follow your messages when choosing your action.
