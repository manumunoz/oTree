Instructions Part 1


Groups, Appearances and Labels

In this experiment you will interact with {{ Constants.others }} players more. At the beginning of the experiment each of you will be assigned to one of two groups: ● or ▲, and will be assigned an appearance (◯ or △) and a label (1 to 7). Your group and appearance are fixed for all 10 rounds in Part 1 while your label will change in each round. There are 3 stages in each round.

Stage 1: Relations

In stage 1, you can propose relations to other players. A relation with another player active if both you and the other player propose to each other. Each proposed relation will cost you {{ Constants.link_cost }} points.

Stage 2: Options

In stage 2, you will choose an option: Up or Down. You will earn points depending on your group, the option you choose, and the number of your active relations choosing the same option as you:

If your group is ● and you:

  • choose Up, you get {{ Constants.liked_gain }} points for each active relation choosing Up (including yourself) and 0 points for any active relation choosing Down

  • choose Down, you get {{ Constants.disliked_gain }} points for each active relation choosing Down (including yourself) and 0 points for any active relation choosing Up

If your group is ▲ and you:

  • choose Down, you get {{ Constants.liked_gain }} points for each active relation choosing Down (including yourself) and 0 points for any active relation choosing Up

  • choose Up, you get {{ Constants.disliked_gain }} points for each active relation choosing Up (including yourself) and 0 points for any active relation choosing Down

Stage 3: Results

In stage 3, you will see how many points you get in that round, which are calculated by the gains of coordinating with your active relations minus the costs of your proposed relations. The border of each player's appearance (◯ or △) shows the option that player chose.

Earnings in the experiment

You will participate for 10 rounds in Part 1 and for 10 rounds in Part 2. In both parts you will interact with the same other {{ Constants.others }} players. At the end of the experiment the computer will randomly select two rounds, one from Part 1 and one from Part 2. The total number of points you got in the selected rounds will determine your payment.
