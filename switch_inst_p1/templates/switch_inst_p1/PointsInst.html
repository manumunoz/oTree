Stage 3: Results

In stage 3, you will see how many points you get in that round, which are calculated by the gains of coordinating with your active relations minus the costs of your proposed relations. The computer will do the calculations for you in each round, but below you can see how the points are calculated in 3 simple steps:


Step 1: Gains from coordination

Gains in each round depend on (i) your group, (ii) the option you pick, and (iii) the number of your active relations choosing the same (including yourself). You do not earn anything with a player if you do not have an active relation together.

If your group is ● and you:

  • choose Up, you get {{ Constants.liked_gain }} points for each active relation choosing Up (including yourself) and 0 points for any active relation choosing Down

  • choose Down, you get {{ Constants.disliked_gain }} points for each active relation choosing Down (including yourself) and 0 points for any active relation choosing Up

If your group is ▲ and you:

  • choose Down, you get {{ Constants.liked_gain }} points for each active relation choosing Down (including yourself) and 0 points for any active relation choosing Up

  • choose Up, you get {{ Constants.disliked_gain }} points for each active relation choosing Up (including yourself) and 0 points for any active relation choosing Down


Step 2: Costs from proposals

Your costs in each round depend only on the number of relations you propose. You pay a cost of 2 points for each proposal you make.


Step 3: Total points

Your points are calculated as the gains from coordinating with your active relations minus the cost of each relation you propose. The examples illustrate this:


Example 1: You are Player 6, your group is ● and you chose Up

1. Gains: You have 6 coordinations (with player 1, 2, 3, 4, 7 and yourself). Your 6 coordinations are multiplied by {{ Constants.liked_gain }} (because your group is ● and you chose Up). So you gain 36 points.

2. Costs: You proposed 6 relations (to players 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 and 7), each costs {{ Constants.link_cost }} points, so you pay 12 points.

3. Total Points: You get 36 in gains minus 12 in costs: 36 - 12 = 24 points in this round.


Example 2: You are Player 1, your group is ▲ and you chose Down

1. Gains: You have 6 coordination (with 5 of your active relations plus yourself). Your 6 coordinations are multiplied by {{ Constants.liked_gain }} (because your group is ▲ and you chose Down). So you gain 36 points.

2. Costs: You proposed 6 relations, so you pay 6 x 2 = 12 points.

3. Total Points: You get 36 in gains minus 12 in costs: 36 - 12 = 24 points in this round.
