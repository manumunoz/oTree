Points: Gains - Costs

In each round you will gain points for every active relation choosing the same option as you. You will pay a cost for each relation you propose. So the total points you earn in a round are the gains from your coordinations minus the costs of your proposals. The computer will do the calculations for you in each round, but below you can see how the points are calculated in 3 simple steps:


Step 1: Calculating Gains from coordinations

Gains in each round depend on (i) your group, (ii) the option you pick, and (iii) the number of your active relations choosing the same. You do not earn anything with those players with whom you do not have an active relation.

If your group is ● and you:

  • choose Up, you get {{ Constants.liked_gain }} points for each active of your connections choosing Up and 0 points for any of your active connections choosing Down

  • choose Down, you get {{ Constants.disliked_gain }} points for each of your active connections choosing Down and 0 points for any of your active connections choosing Up

If your group is ▲ and you:

  • choose Down, you get {{ Constants.liked_gain }} points for each active connection choosing Down and 0 points for any of your active connections choosing Up

  • choose Up, you get {{ Constants.disliked_gain }} points for each active connection choosing Up and 0 points for any of your active connections choosing Down


Step 2: Calculating Costs from proposals

Your costs in each round depend only on the number of relations you propose. You pay a cost of 2 points for each proposal you make.


Step 3: Calculating points

Your points are calculated as the gains from coordinating with your active relations minus the cost of each relation you propose. The examples illustrate this:


Example 1: You are Player 6 and your group is ●

1. Gains: You chose action Up and have coordinated with 5 of your active connections. So you have 6 coordinations (one with yourself and one with player 1, 2, 3, 4, and 7). Your 6 coordinations are multiplied by 6 (because your group is ● and you chose Up). So you gain 36 points.

2. Costs: You proposed 6 relations, each costs 2 points, so you pay 12 points.

3. Total Points: You get 36 in gains minus 12 in costs: 36 - 12 = 24 points in this round.


Example 2: You are Player 1 and your group is ▲

1. Gains: You chose action Down and have 6 coordination (with yourself and with 5 of your active relations). Your 6 coordinations are multiplied by 6 (because your group is ▲ and you chose Down). So you gain 36 points.

2. Costs: You proposed 6 relations, so you pay 6 x 2 = 8 points.

3. Total Points: You get 36 in gains minus 12 in costs: 36 - 12 = 24 points in this round.
