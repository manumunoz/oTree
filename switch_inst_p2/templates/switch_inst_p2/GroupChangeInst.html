In Part 2 you will participate for 10 rounds with the same {{ Constants.others }} other players from Part 1. While, each round in Part 1 had 3 stages, in Part 2 each round has 4 stages: Group choice (Stage 1), Relations (Stage 2), Options (Stage 3) and Results (Stage 4).

The Relations stage, Options stage and Results stage are the same as in Part 1. The Group Choice stage is new and is explained below.

Stage 1: Group Choice
At the beginning of each round the number of players in each group is the same as in Part 1: 4 ● and 3 ▲ . In the Group Choice stage each player will choose whether to change its group (and appearance) from Part 1 or stay in it (see example). You make this choice in each round.

If a player in group ● stays in the same group, he will earn points as always. That is, {{ Constants.liked_gain }} points for each coordination on Up and {{ Constants.disliked_gain}} points for each coordination on Down.

However, if a player in group ● changes to group ▲, he will earn points as a ▲. That is, {{ Constants.liked_gain}} points for each coordination on Down and {{ Constants.disliked_gain}} points for each coordination on Up.

Similarly, if a player in group ▲ changes to group ●, he will earn points as a ●, but if he chooses to stay in group ▲, he will continue earning points as a ▲.

Stage 2: Relations
This stage is the same as in Part 1. Each player can propose relations to others and pays a cost of 2 points for each relation proposed.

Stage 3: Options
This stage is the same as in Part 1. Each player picks one of two options: Up or Down and earns points by the number of his/her active connections choosing the same option.

Stage 4: Results
This stage is the same as in Part 1. Each player is informed of the number of total points he gets in the round. This is calculated by the gains from coordination (which depend on the group chosen in the current round, the option picked, and the number of coordinations with active relations) minus the costs of proposing relations to others.
